ENGINE (baseline, this commit): DEADLOCKS -- all four drive ONTO the junction (enterJunction

ROOT CAUSE. The four straight movements form a directed right-before-left 4-cycle (each yields to the
movement on its right): NC->CS (link 1) yields to WC->CE (13); WC->CE (13) yields to SC->CN (9);
SC->CN (9) yields to EC->CW (5); EC->CW (5) yields to NC->CS (1). No movement has priority over its
own foe, and all arrive together, so the willPass pre-pass resolves WillPass=false for ALL four
(each is braking-to-yield). In the real PlanMovements pass the crossing gate's
`foeYieldsThisStep = !foe.WillPass` is then TRUE for every ego (its foe "won't pass"), so ALL four
proceed onto the junction simultaneously -> mutual mid-junction gridlock.

SUMO breaks the symmetry because willPass is computed SEQUENTIALLY per vehicle during planMove
(MSLink::setApproaching-before-opened()): the lower-link-index axis (N-S, links 0-3) registers its
pass first, and the E-W vehicles read that settled willPass=true and stay stop-line-held. Replicating
this in the engine faithfully requires a DETERMINISTIC (canonical, NOT thread-order) resolution of the
conflict cycle -- effectively selecting a maximal set of mutually-non-conflicting movements with the
highest static priority (the N-S axis here) to pass while the rest yield. That is a conflict-graph
independent-set / arrival-time priority resolution, a new mechanism with HIGH regression risk to the
~11 committed crossing scenarios (08/11/26/27/29/31/32/38/39/40 + 44), and needs a SUMO
MSLink_DEBUG_OPENED trace to nail the exact per-step tie-break for @1e-3 parity. It is its own rung,
NOT landed here. See C4-VII-REMAINING.md "#2 Symmetric arrival-time RoW" for the full fix design.
